# Mobile Pilot Rollout Decision Summary

## Purpose

The mobile app now produces a short rollout decision summary for the rollout lead.

This is the fast answer to:

- can this device support expansion to the next wave?
- should we hold and monitor?
- do we need to investigate evidence gaps first?
- has this device crossed into rollback or escalation posture?

## What the summary combines

The decision summary blends:

- current readiness signoff
- recovery desk posture
- operator action center recommendation
- active evidence completion
- recent archived-session trend

That keeps the rollout lead from stitching those answers together manually.

## Verdicts

- `READY TO EXPAND`
  - current posture is clean
  - recent archived evidence is healthy
  - the device is in a strong position for controlled rollout expansion

- `HOLD AND MONITOR`
  - the device is usable
  - but there is still not enough depth, history, or cleanliness to widen rollout confidently

- `INVESTIGATE BEFORE EXPAND`
  - evidence gaps still exist
  - or recent archived sessions closed with attention signs
  - rollout should pause until those issues are reviewed

- `ROLLBACK AND ESCALATE`
  - the device is in a blocked or incident posture
  - wave expansion should stop until support or rollback handling is completed

## When to use it

Use `Copy decision summary` when:

- the rollout lead wants one pasteable device verdict
- a wave review needs a concise recommendation
- you want the current posture plus archive trend without copying every underlying report

Use the larger evidence pack when:

- audit or support needs the deeper report set
- you need the full readiness, recovery, and snapshot detail

## Recommended use

1. Resolve normal smoke, readiness, and recovery steps first.
2. Let the device accumulate at least one real archived session when possible.
3. Use `Copy decision summary` before making a wave expansion call.
4. If the decision says `INVESTIGATE BEFORE EXPAND` or `ROLLBACK AND ESCALATE`, stop widening rollout until the underlying issue is addressed.
