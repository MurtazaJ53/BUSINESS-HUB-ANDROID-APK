# Business Hub Plan Packaging Sheet

## Purpose

This sheet explains Business Hub plans in outcome language for onboarding, support, and sales conversations.

It should be used instead of talking through hidden system capability.

## Starter

Best for:

- one shop that needs fast daily selling

Client gets:

- POS
- inventory lookup
- customer balances
- recent receipts
- simple daily business view

Promise:

- run the counter cleanly without extra complexity

Do not position Starter as:

- incomplete ERP
- limited trial of every advanced module

## Growth

Best for:

- a shop that now needs team and operating controls

Client gets everything in Starter plus:

- expenses
- attendance
- supplier-ready product context
- stronger business review

Promise:

- keep store operations under control without moving into full finance complexity

## Pro

Best for:

- a business that needs stronger reporting and deeper operational control

Client gets everything in Growth plus:

- advanced reports
- finance-heavy summaries
- purchase workflow context
- selected advanced operational controls

Promise:

- run Business Hub with richer management visibility while still keeping the client-facing product curated

## Positioning rules

Say:

- faster selling
- cleaner stock control
- stronger customer follow-up
- better owner visibility
- deeper controls when the business is ready

Do not say:

- more DocTypes
- more ERP forms
- backend access
- finance serializer fields

## Upgrade triggers

### Starter to Growth

Upgrade when the client says:

- we need expenses
- we need staff attendance
- we want more day-to-day control

### Growth to Pro

Upgrade when the client says:

- we need deeper reporting
- we need richer finance-aware summaries
- we need more advanced back-office discipline

## Packaging acceptance

This sheet is ready when:

- sales can explain it in under 2 minutes
- support can reuse the same language
- product screens match the same promises
